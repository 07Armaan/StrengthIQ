Your body, explained

STRENGTH IS DATA WEARING MUSCLE

Every kilogram you lift is the output of a biological calculation — one that takes in your age, your hormonal environment, the supplements you take, the weeks you've trained, and the weight your body has gained or lost. These aren't random variables. They are the exact inputs that determine how much stronger you'll be next month.


Your initial body weight sets the baseline for how your frame handles progressive overload. The difference in weight over the training period signals whether your body has been in an anabolic environment — gaining lean mass — or cutting, which shifts the strength trajectory entirely.

Supplement science
Supplements & weeks

THE RIGHT COMPOUND OVER ENOUGH TIME

Creatine monohydrate increases phosphocreatine stores in muscle tissue, directly fueling high-intensity contractions and producing measurable strength increases within 3–4 weeks. Protein supplements maintain a positive nitrogen balance when dietary intake falls short. Pre-workouts drive training intensity, indirectly amplifying the stimulus for adaptation.


But supplements only work over time. Our model accounts for training weeks as a core variable — because 4 weeks of creatine and 16 weeks of creatine produce fundamentally different strength outcomes, and our prediction captures that curve.

The 7 input variables

WHAT THE MODEL READS ABOUT YOU

🧬
Age

Hormonal output, recovery capacity, and neuromuscular efficiency all shift with age. Younger trainees typically show higher peak strength gains; the model adjusts baseline expectations accordingly.

⚧
Gender

Testosterone levels, lean mass baseline, and anabolic response differ by gender. These differences influence the rate and ceiling of strength gain, which the model accounts for in its output.

💊
Supplement

The type of supplement used — creatine, protein, pre-workout, or none — directly affects strength output. Each compound has a distinct mechanism and magnitude of effect on neuromuscular performance.

📅
Weeks

Training duration determines how far along the adaptation curve you are. Short blocks produce rapid initial gains; longer durations reflect sustained progressive overload and compound adaptation.

⚖️
Initial body weight

Your starting body weight sets the absolute load context. Heavier athletes often move more total weight; lighter athletes may show higher relative percentage gains. Both patterns are modelled.

🎯
Primary benefit

Your stated training goal — muscle gain, fat loss, endurance, or strength — shapes your programming and nutrition, which in turn influences what kind of strength adaptations the body prioritizes.

📈
Weight difference found

The change in body weight over the training period is a strong proxy for the anabolic environment — net muscle gain, fat loss, or both. A positive weight difference in a strength context typically correlates with higher predicted gains.

Beyond the gym

STRENGTH GROWS WHEN YOU REST

The training session is the stimulus. Recovery is where strength actually builds. Muscle protein synthesis peaks in the 24–48 hours after a session, during which sleep, nutrition, and stress management determine whether the body adapts upward or simply repairs.


Our model captures this through the weeks variable and the weight difference — both of which implicitly encode whether your lifestyle outside the gym is supporting the work you're doing inside it. A 12-week block with consistent weight gain tells a very different story than 12 weeks at the same weight.
